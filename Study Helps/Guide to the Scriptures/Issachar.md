 A son of Jacob and Leah in the Old Testament ([[Old Testament/Genesis/Genesis 30#^verse-17|Gen. 30:17-18]]; [[Old Testament/Genesis/Genesis 35#^verse-23|35:23]]; [[Old Testament/Genesis/Genesis 46#^verse-13|46:13]]). His descendants became one of the twelve tribes of Israel.

## The tribe of Issachar

 Jacob's blessing to Issachar is found in [[Old Testament/Genesis/Genesis 49#^verse-14|Genesis 49:14-15]]. After settling in Canaan, the tribe received some of the richest land of Palestine, including the plain of Esdraelon. Within Issachar's borders were several important places in Jewish history, for example, Carmel, Megiddo, Dothan, Gilboa, Jezreel, Tabor, and Nazareth ([[Old Testament/Joshua/Joshua 19#^verse-17|Josh. 19:17-23]]).
