 In scripture, an open area of land used for cultivation or pasture. It often symbolizes the world and its peoples.

 The field is the world, [[New Testament/Matthew/Matthew 13#^verse-38|Matt. 13:38]].

 The kingdom of heaven is like unto treasure hid in a field, [[New Testament/Matthew/Matthew 13#^verse-44|Matt. 13:44]].

 I beheld a large and spacious field, [[Book of Mormon/1 Nephi/1 Nephi 8#^verse-9|1 Ne. 8:9, 20]].

 The field was ripe, [[Book of Mormon/Alma/Alma 26#^verse-5|Alma 26:5]].

 The field is white already to harvest, [[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 4#^verse-4|D&C 4:4]] ([[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 6#^verse-3|D&C 6:3]]; [[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 11#^verse-3|11:3]]; [[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 12#^verse-3|12:3]]; [[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 14#^verse-3|14:3]]; [[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 31#^verse-4|31:4]]; [[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 33#^verse-3|33:3, 7]]).

 The field was the world, [[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 86#^verse-1|D&C 86:1-2]].

 I will liken these kingdoms unto a man having a field, [[Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants/Doctrine and Covenants 88#^verse-51|D&C 88:51]].
